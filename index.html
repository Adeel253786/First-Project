INTRODUCTION

Chocolate is one of the most popular food types and flavors in the world, and many foodstuffs involving chocolate exist, particularly desserts, including cakes, pudding, mousse, brownies, and chocolate chip cookies. Many candies are filled with or coated with sweetened chocolate. Chocolate bars, either made of solid chocolate or other ingredients coated in chocolate, are eaten as snacks. Gifts of chocolate molded into different shapes (such as eggs and hearts) are traditional on certain Western holidays, including Christmas, Easter, Valentine's Day, and Hanukkah. Chocolate is also used in cold and hot beverages, such as chocolate milk and hot chocolate.

USES AND BENEFITS OF CHOCOLATE

Brain Function:

The caffeine and other natural stimulants in chocolate can provide a short-term boost to brain function.

Heart Health:

Chocolate can help keep your heart healthy. Chocolate can help prevent liver damage.

Cosmetics:

Cocoa's antioxidants are used in cosmetics to delay signs of aging, burn fat deposits.

Skin Health:

The flavonols in dark chocolate can protect the skin against sun damage, improve blood flow, and increase skin density and hydration

Dessert:

Chocolate is a popular ingredient in sweet treats like cakes, cookies, cupcakes, ice cream, and hot chocolate.

Blood Pressure:

The flavanols in cocoa work like blood pressure-lowering drugs called ACE inhibitors.

Coca Bean Fruit & Seed

The cacao pod is a berry-like fruit that grows on the trunk and branches of the cacao tree. The pod is ovoid, 6–12 in long, 3–4 in wide, and weighs about 1 lb when ripe. The pod has a rough, leathery rind and is filled with a sweet, mucilaginous pulp. The seeds inside the pod are called cocoa beans. Each pod contains 16–60 cocoa beans. The beans are almond-shaped, soft, and pale lavender to dark brownish purple in color.
